Climate change is the defining challenge of our time, and we need a bold, nationwide mobilization plan to avert the consequences to our planet. Add your name now if you agree that our next president needs to make this a priority.

A lot of ideas to fight climate change have been making the headlines lately, including the soundbite-friendly “Green New Deal.” But John refuses to campaign on an easy fix or on promises he can’t execute on. As he likes to say, great ideas are a dime a dozen: what matters is what you do. And with the clock running out for our planet, nothing matters more than what we can actually get done in Washington.

John’s the only candidate in this primary who has actually brought people—often people on polar opposite ends of the spectrum—together to pursue bold, concrete solutions to protect our planet. Having done it in Colorado, he’s ready to do it for our nation.

Fundamental to John’s approach is collaboration. We’re not going to solve the climate crisis with massive expansions of government, or with extreme rhetoric that triggers backlash from our opponents and costs us even more valuable time to take action. Just as an example: As Governor of Colorado, by taking the opposite tack and bringing opposing parties to the table, John passed the strictest methane regulations in the country—and got the oil and gas industry to pay for it.

We have to work together to fight this crisis: Democrats, Republicans, government, the private sector, environmental activists, the energy industry, developing and developed nations alike. It starts with:

  • Rejoining the Paris Agreement, re-forging our relationships with our global climate partners, and building climate goals into trade agreements and even foreign aid and military agreements

  • Treating the private sector as indispensable partners for climate action and boldly unleashing market forces to discover the technological and infrastructural solutions we need

  • Putting a scientific approach first: Solving this crisis will take listening to and learning from the best minds from every corner of the world. As the only trained scientist in the primary, John will make sure all climate change efforts are based on the best available data and worldwide scientific consensus.
