About Rep. Tulsi Gabbard:

Tulsi is the first female combat veteran ever to run for the presidency, and was the first female combat veteran ever elected to Congress, along with Tammy Duckworth. Currently a Major in the Army National Guard, she has served for over 16 years and deployed twice to the Middle East.

She is a Democrat who was first elected to Congress in 2012 and has served for over 6 years on the Homeland Security, Foreign Affairs, and Armed Services Committees in Congress.

Tulsi Gabbard was Vice Chair of the Democratic National Committee from 2013 until she resigned in 2016 to endorse Bernie Sanders in his bid for President.

Tulsi Gabbard’s campaign for President is powered completely by people. She does not accept campaign contributions from corporations, lobbyists, or political action committees.

Tulsi Gabbard was born a US citizen on April 12, 1981, in American Samoa. Her family moved to Hawaii when she was 2 years old, where she grew up. As is typical of many residents of Hawaii, she is of mixed ethnicity. She is of Asian, Caucasian, and Polynesian descent.
